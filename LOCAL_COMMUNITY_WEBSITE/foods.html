PEKAN KUNDASANG SIGNATURE FOODS

HINAVA

Hinava is a traditional native dish of the Kadazan-Dusun people in the state of Sabah. It is made from fish and mixed with lime juice, bird's eye chili, sliced shallots, and grated ginger. Hinava is a popular dish during celebrations like the Kaamatan Festival but is also enjoyed as a daily delicacy. Its tangy, fresh, and slightly spicy flavor makes it a beloved part of Sabah's culinary heritage.

SAMBAL TUHAU

Sambal Tuhau is a traditional side dish prepared by the natives in Sabah, especially by the Dusun who lives in the interior. It's perfect to accompany local rice wine or beer and to treat hangover. is a traditional Sabahan condiment made from tuhau, which is the shoots of the wild ginger plant (Etlingera coccinea). It is a popular dish among the Kadazan-Dusun community in Sabah. The tuhau is finely chopped and mixed with ingredients like chili, garlic, and lime juice, creating a tangy, spicy, and slightly pungent sambal.

LINOPOT

Linopot is a traditional dish from the Kadazan-Dusun people of Sabah, Malaysia. It is essentially rice wrapped in leaves, typically banana leaves or tarap leaves, which give the dish its distinctive aroma and flavor. The rice used can be plain or mixed with other ingredients such as sweet potatoes, yams, or tapioca for added texture and taste.
