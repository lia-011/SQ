FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTION

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions to help you learn more about the area.

  • Pekan Kundasang is known for its cool weather, breathtaking views of Mount Kinabalu, and its proximity to various natural and historical sites.

  • The best way to get around is by car, whether it's a rental or a taxi, as public transportation options are limited. Many visitors also opt for guided tours.

  • Yes, while climbing Mount Kinabalu usually starts from the Kinabalu Park entrance, you can base yourself in Kundasang as it's nearby, and the park is a popular stop for those preparing to hike.

  • While some attractions, like hiking trails, may benefit from a guide, many spots such as Desa Dairy Farm or local markets can be explored independently.

  • Pekan Kundasang is about 2 to 3 hours by car from Kota Kinabalu, making it an easily accessible weekend getaway.

  • You may encounter wildlife such as various bird species, primates, and endemic species in the surrounding forests, especially in Kinabalu National Park.

  • Yes, families can enjoy the cooler climate, scenic views, and family-friendly attractions like the Desa Dairy Farm, Lavender Garden, and nature parks.

  • Yes, most of the town has mobile network coverage, but it can be patchy in more remote areas. It is always a good idea to check with your provider about roaming services.

  • Yes, there are farms around Kundasang where you can pick fresh strawberries, making it a fun activity for visitors. Some farms also sell homemade strawberry products like jam or juice.

  • A 2-3 day stay is usually ideal for exploring the main attractions, enjoying the natural beauty, and experiencing local culture. Longer stays are great if you want to do more hiking or take it slow.
